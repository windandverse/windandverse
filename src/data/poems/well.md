---
title: "Well"
author: "Roméon"
---

Are you well?  
They ask with a smile  
Nothing to tell  
I say from a mile, Emotionally  
I'm stuck in a well  
Filling up with tears torn from my eyes  
Blood drawn from my leaking core  
Sweat cried from chasing you in my mind  
I should have learned to swim  
Instead, I held your grip  
Now I can't help but slip  

As our fingers aren't crossed, just mine  
Wishing that you stay till dawn  
But maybe dusk is fine  
I don't want to see who I am without you  
If time heals all wounds, I'll need an eternity  
This love was never blind, built on hypocrisy and lies  
Drifting with ocean tides  

Now on this barren shore, that I dare call home  
My skin shrank to accommodate your absence  
Climbed up the well, no rope, no slope  
No longer drenched in the tears that I shed  
That was three years ago.  
Now, you want us back?  
On some sob story, I would have loved  
But I don't want to go back to  
When I asked 'y' at the end of the da-y  
When every month was May  
May you find your way  
Back into my miserable embrace  

But my hands forgot how to hold you  
My mouth forgot how to call you mine  
My dumb heart never forgot  
But now the only forever I can offer  
Is a place in my heart  
Where the fire died and the well dried  
Because I'm not well, not anymore

~ Roméon🫗 ~
