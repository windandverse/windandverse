---
title: "The Thornless Rose"
author: "Roméon"
---

Thornless rose  
If I saw a thornless rose  
I'd have found perfection  
Sight of painless allure  
I'd hold on, never let go  
so I did, but when there were  
none on the exterior  
the interior made into a bush  
we couldn't beat around  
walked right through  
Smiling like mad men  

the spikes, a diadem  
It's weight screaming unjilted  
We danced  
between the gold and the blue  
With anything we could find  
sharp or blunt we lived for scars  
The art of trust and the labour of hope  
We bled, pleaded and yet  
wouldn't excoriate each other  
because on either end  
Lied an anguished soul  
hailing such a suffering  

that death draws inspiration  
Contemptible to the core  
Each I love you, Ironwork to the dangeon  
the dark cells that harbour our essence  
A thornless rose, The beauty on the outside  
a juxtaposition of the pain within  
there's no perfection, only delusion  
we were the mole of our undoing  
It's grueling to swim in blood  
when each drop suppresses  
passions we shared  
So we drown in red and still  

I'd rather die in your arms  
than far from the touch  
of your cold embrace  
known to me more  
than the warmth of my heart

~ Roméon🥀 ~
