---
title: "Rain drop"
author: "Roméon"
---

From as long as I can remember  
I have never been alone on my own  
From creation to Noah's flood  
I don't evolve I revolve  
changing, with nothing of my own  

If you dissect Niagara i'll be there  
on the end of a shark's tail  
shouldering the trees of life  
The Amazon knows me by name  
No where specific in the pacific  
I kiss more than I can tell  
find me under the wishing well  
on the end of a fire hose  
dew on a field of rose  
Murderer, Nourisher, bringer and taker  
In the sewers beneath cities  
Puddles to splash and pass  
I have been all around the world  
Still no where I fit in  

But that passing moment knocking on her apartment  
drooling on her window without judgement  
The lights inside ignite all my imperfections  
I have never been this insignificant nor happier  
I am infinite but this is forever  
She sees me racing my kin  
Unreachable, invincible, I found my flow  
Beyond life I am aesthetics  
our harmonious cry for salvation  
Makes life a little worth while

~ Roméon🌧️ ~
