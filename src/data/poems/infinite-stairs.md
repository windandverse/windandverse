---
title: "Infinite Stairs"
author: "Roméon"
---

The meaning of life  
Is meaningless itself  
an infinite staircase  
That wanders,  
circles and rises  
more stops than steps  
flaws than floors  
Nothing is perfect  
faulty, dirty, greedy  
Not where to start  
where or when to finish  
creaky wood, existing loudly  
paws on marble, hiding  

To find yourself  
you create him or her or them  
or surrender to the system  
neither is wrong,  
neither is freedom  
meaning is limited  
life is edited  
Everyone has their summit  
step by step  
none know the sum of it  

It's okay to leave,  
and be left behind  
The stops we make  
define us, refine us  
design us and confine us  

Some steps are dark  
some are too bright  
some are just right  
perfection is sleight  
a comfort in make up  

We chase echoes  
rush on some steps  
We map the voices  
drag on others  
Just stories to tell  

We trip, we fall  
We hurt and break  
But it is never enough  

Infinity is possibility  
maybe it's a void, uncertainty  
Always vulnerability  
stares saying you are slow  
dares that pull you close  
peers begging for patience  

Sometimes there's banisters  
Peace of mind  
More often there's none  
We are afraid  

some people get a lift  
others need to be lifted  
We are not the same  
That was never a shame  

It's okay to sit on the steps  
Have a drink with a friend  
But there's infinity out there  
And no one lives on the stairs

~ Roméon🪜 ~
